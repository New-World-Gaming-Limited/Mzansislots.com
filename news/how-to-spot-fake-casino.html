How to Spot a Fake Online Casino

Safety | 5 March 2026 | By MzansiSlots Editorial Team

Not every online casino is trustworthy. Rogue operators target players with fake bonuses, rigged games, and delayed or refused withdrawals. Knowing the warning signs can protect your money and personal data from fraudulent sites.

Check for a Valid Gambling Licence

The most important verification step is checking whether the casino holds a legitimate gambling licence. In South Africa, licensed operators display their provincial gambling board licence details in the website footer. International casinos should hold licences from recognised authorities such as the Malta Gaming Authority, UK Gambling Commission, or Curacao eGaming.

Do not take the casino's word for it. Visit the licensing authority's website and search their register to confirm the licence is genuine and current. Fake casinos often display counterfeit licence logos or reference expired licences.

Unrealistic Bonus Offers

If a bonus sounds too good to be true, it almost certainly is. Legitimate casinos offer welcome bonuses typically ranging from R25 free to 150% deposit matches. If a site promises R50,000 free with no wagering requirements, that is a major red flag.

Read the terms and conditions carefully. Legitimate bonuses always have wagering requirements (typically 20x to 40x), maximum withdrawal limits, and game contribution weightings. If you cannot find clear bonus terms, avoid the casino.

Poor Website Quality and Missing Information

Professional casinos invest in their online presence. Warning signs include spelling and grammar errors throughout the site, broken links, low-resolution graphics, and a lack of essential pages such as Terms and Conditions, Privacy Policy, and Responsible Gambling information.

Check for a physical address, contact email, and live chat support. Legitimate operators are transparent about who they are and how to reach them. If the only contact option is a generic email form with no response guarantee, proceed with caution.

Limited or Unusual Payment Options

Trustworthy casinos offer well-known payment methods including bank transfers, credit/debit cards, and established e-wallets. Be wary of casinos that only accept cryptocurrency or obscure payment processors, as these can be difficult to trace if something goes wrong.

Check withdrawal policies before depositing. Rogue casinos often have excessively long withdrawal processing times (beyond 5 business days), very high minimum withdrawal amounts, or hidden fees. Read player reviews on independent forums to see if others have experienced withdrawal problems.

How to Verify a Casino Is Safe

Follow this checklist before registering at any new online casino:

  • Verify the gambling licence on the issuing authority's website
  • Read the full Terms and Conditions, especially for bonuses and withdrawals
  • Check independent review sites and player forums for feedback
  • Test customer support responsiveness before depositing
  • Ensure the site uses SSL encryption (look for the padlock icon in your browser)
  • Confirm the casino offers responsible gambling tools

If any of these checks raise concerns, choose a different casino. There are plenty of reputable options available to South African players. Your safety should always come first.
