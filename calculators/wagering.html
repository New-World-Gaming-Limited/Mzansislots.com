Understanding Wagering Requirements

Everything South African players need to know about bonus wagering before claiming an offer.

What is a Wagering Requirement?

A wagering requirement (also called a playthrough requirement) is a condition attached to most casino bonuses. It specifies how many times you must bet the bonus amount before any winnings can be withdrawn.

For example: if you receive a R500 bonus with a 30x wagering requirement, you must place a total of R15,000 in bets before your balance becomes withdrawable. The operator sets this to ensure you actually play rather than immediately withdrawing the bonus.

  • Higher multiplier = harder to clear
  • Some apply to both deposit and bonus combined
  • Free spin winnings usually have separate requirements
  • Time limits typically range from 7 to 30 days
  • Game contributions vary (slots usually 100%, live casino 10-20%)

How to Calculate if a Bonus is Worth It

The expected value (EV) calculation tells you whether a bonus is mathematically profitable on average. Here is the three-step method:

Total Wagering = Bonus x Multiplier
e.g. R500 x 30 = R15,000

Expected Loss = Total Wagering x (1 - RTP)
e.g. R15,000 x 0.04 = R600

Net EV = Bonus - Expected Loss
e.g. R500 - R600 = -R100 (-EV)

If Net EV is positive, the bonus adds value on average. If negative, you are expected to lose more than the bonus is worth. Use the calculator above to run these numbers for any offer.

What is RTP and Why Does it Matter?

RTP (Return to Player) is the percentage of all money wagered that a slot pays back to players over millions of spins. A 96% RTP means the game returns R96 for every R100 bet on average, giving the house a 4% edge.

When clearing a wagering requirement, your expected loss per rand wagered is equal to the house edge. Playing a 96% RTP slot costs you 4c per rand, while a 92% RTP slot costs 8c per rand, doubling your losses on the same wagering requirement.

  • 96% to 98% RTP: player-friendly range
  • 94% to 96% RTP: industry average
  • Below 93% RTP: high house edge, avoid for bonus play
  • Spina Zonke games average 92-94% RTP
  • Crash games like Aviator often run at 97% RTP

Bonus Terms to Check Before You Claim

Beyond the wagering multiplier, several other terms determine whether a bonus is genuinely valuable. Always check these before claiming any South African casino offer:

  • Minimum deposit: The amount you must deposit to qualify
  • Maximum bonus cap: The maximum rand value of the bonus
  • Time limit: Days you have to clear the wagering requirement
  • Max bet rule: Maximum stake per spin (usually R25 to R50)
  • Game restrictions: Which games count toward wagering
  • Cashout cap: Maximum you can withdraw from bonus winnings
  • Bonus applies to: Bonus only vs deposit and bonus combined
